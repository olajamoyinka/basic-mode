CAUSES OF ROAD ACCIDENTS

Accidents occur through one of the following factor:

  1. The driver
  2. The vehicle
  3. The environmen
DRIVER

The driver constitutes the major factor, because the vehicle and the environment can hardly cause the accident.

  1. Lack of knowledge of road signs, illiteracy e.g. not able to read and interprete traffic road signs.
  2. Vehicle abuse, over confidence, wrong trifurcation, errors of judgement, claiming right of way.
  3. Poor visibility, failure to use driving mirrors, impatient, wrongful overtaking and night driving with defective vision.
VEHICLE
  1. Unroadworthy vehicles.
  2. Brake failure.
  3. Faulty tie rod ends.
  4. Broken down vehicles without 'C' caution sign placed.
  5. Vehicles which emits excessive fumes and smokes.

ROAD ACCIDENTS PREVENTION

  1. Motorist should have knowledge of road traffic signs, e.g. how to read and interpret the signs.
  2. All drivers should not drive when fatigued,sleepy, under the influence of alcohol, annoyance,, concentration is less and medically unfit
  3. Avoid dangerous overtaking at bends, junction, on brow ofa hill.
  4. Do not make U-turn where doing so will constitute danger to other road users.
  5. Do not take any sign for granted

QUALITIES OF A GOOD DRIVER

  • Concentration
  • Observation
  • Anticipation
  • Vehicle sympathy and care
  • Punctuality
